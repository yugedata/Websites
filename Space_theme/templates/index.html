10/30/2020
I recently completed an elevator pitch for my Creativity and Innovation class at South Alabama. The topic is; why Mobile County should expand their recycling program to include pick-up recycling options. Hope you like it! “My name is Abigale McCormick, and I am a marketing student at South Alabama, aspiring to focus my marketing career on environmental activism. I am sure you have heard the phrase, “reduce, reuse, recycle” Today, I want to focus on the last part of that, Recycle. Recycling is a crucial part of protecting the unique ecosystem here in Coastal Alabama. Currently, Mobile County only offers drop-off recycling, which is a great start, but; the time and energy it takes to use the current process deter non-recycling households from conforming. You might be saying to yourself that the drop-off recycling is plenty or not everyone has to recycle, but what if I told you Alabama alone has over 170 landfills that accept 19 million tons of JUST out-of-state garbage each year. If that still does not drive your enthusiasm for this project, the 23 endangered species in Mobile and Baldwin County should. Trash winds up in our water, and as it breaks down over time, toxins make their way into natural wildlife and our homes. Not to mention the impact our city cleanliness has on tourism. I understand that implementing a pick-up recycling program would be costly. Fortunately, Alabama offers the Alabama Recycling Fund Grant, which provides funding for cities implementing recycling programs. Given that; Mobile County already has a majority of the recycling equipment, the main expenses would be employee wages, recycling trucks, and increased PPE. The increase in operations would allow for mobile to create upwards of 100 jobs at the beginning stages of the project. Helping to stimulate the local economy and community well-being. Now I ask that you take this proposal to those in the Mobile City Sanitation department and instruct them to file for the Alabama Recycling Fund Grant. Thank you for your time and, have a good day.” -Abigale McCormick
